People often ask, "Why should anyone care about the rescue dogs in Spain, when so many dogs in The Netherlands need homes too?"


We tell them this: "Because in Spain stray animal have a small percentage of being in a forever home". In Spain dogs are abandon after they have done there jobs as; hunters, puppy factory or dog fighting. Some stray dogs also have the bad luck that there family don't want them anymore, so they get dumped on the side of the road.


There aren't rescue and cruelty prevention groups such as those in The Netherlands to serve as advocates for these sweet pups. Spanish strays are kicked, beaten, ran over by cars, and left for dead. They wander the streets searching for food, most of them skeletal. And if these dogs have figured out how cruel people can be, they stay in deserted areas, searching for whatever bits of food they can find, trembling and running away from anyone that tries to come near them.


And if they do have the chance of being in a home, many of them are attached to a three-foot chain, next to a dog house that is way too small, that they can barely reach because of how they are chained. They are covered in mud, and left there to watch the cars fly by them every day ... day in, and day out. No feeling of love or warmth from their owner.r


We can sum up the sentiment in Spain in one analogy: If you see a dog walking the streets in The Netherlands, your first thought is usually “Oh no, someone’s dog got out!” Whereas in Spain, it’s “Oh look, another forgotten, stray dog ... with no place to call home ... maybe we should call the dog catcher and get rid of it.”
